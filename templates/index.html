=== Project Overview ===

Welcome to Ask AI Abe, your digital legal assistant powered by state-of-the-art artificial intelligence. Abe is designed to simplify access to legal information, bridging the gap between complex legal jargon and everyday language.

Abe is grounded on GPT-4's advanced language model and is specialized in interpreting the California Legal Code. With Abe, you can ask any well-structured legal question and expect a comprehensible and accurate response rooted in the legal code. Abe's primary function is to provide legal information, making the vast world of laws and regulations more accessible to everyone.

To utilize this tool, simply head to the playground, pose your legal question, and let Abe assist you with relevant information. While Abe is powerful, it's essential to remember that he is not a replacement for professional legal advice. Abe serves as a gateway to legal knowledge, offering a first step in understanding legal matters.

=== Project Goals of Ask AI Abe ===

  • Harness the power of GPT-4 and cutting edge prompt engineering techniques to revolutionize legal question answering for both law professionals and everyday citizens.
  • Improve legal question answering with generative AI by dynamically expanding user search queries, reducing the need for followup questions.
  • Optimize accuracy for high dimensional document search and summary using vector embeddings and adversarial LLM interactions to assist users in better defining their requests.
  • Legitimize the use of LLMs and generative AI in legal applications by including direct legal citations, statutory source text, and official weblinks in generated answers.
  • Empower citizens by eliminating monetary and technological barriers so they can access legal information and better understand the laws that govern them.

=== How to Utilize Abe ===

How do I ask Abe a legal question?

Visit the playground and pose any legal question. Abe will respond based on the relevant information in the California Legal Code.

What type of legal questions can Abe answer?

Abe is equipped to address any well-phrased legal question that is rooted in the legal code. For instance:

Can I smoke cannabis?

Which legal documents is Abe familiar with?

Abe comprehends the entire California State legal code. Rather than being specifically trained or fine-tuned on this data, he utilizes underlying algorithms to reference relevant legal sections as guidelines for his responses.

While Abe operates under the assumption that all questions are within the jurisdiction of California, he can include references to specific local or federal legislation in his answers if they're part of the California legal code.

Plans are in place to soon incorporate all US States and Federal Code and Regulations. The priority is to enhance the accuracy, speed, utility, and authenticity of Abe's responses before broadening the covered jurisdictions.

Plaintext Question: What legal actions do I have if my landlord sells my lease 3 months before my current lease ends and is evicting?

User’s Rights and Privileges when Tenant Sells Lease and Proceeds with Eviction

TLDR

As a tenant, you hold various rights when faced with an eviction following the sale of your lease by the landlord. These rights, as prescribed by the California Civil, Financial, and Code of Civil Procedure, allow for the entitlement of contract damages due to the landlord’s breach, notice of termination requirements, right to file claim for damages, among others.

Right to Contract Damages Due to Tenant’s Breach (Cal. CIV § 1995.320)

Specifics about Contract Damages

Contractual damages can result from the tenant’s breach of lease agreement. You can seek these damages as a remedy. (Cal. CIV § 1995.320)

Notice of Termination (Cal. CIV § 1946)

Advanced Notice Requirement

You must receive a written notice of termination at least 30 days prior to eviction. This notification could be through certified or registered mail. (Cal. CIV § 1946)

Requirement for Notice Delivery

The legal documentation provides a guideline on how this notice will be delivered. (Cal. CIV § 1946)

Restriction on Transfer (Cal. CIV § 1995.310)

Right to seek Contract Damages Due to Landlord’s Breach

If your lease restricts transfer without your consent, and the landlord denies this consent, then you can again seek contract damages due to the landlord’s breach. (Cal. CIV § 1995.310)

Claim for Damages Resulting from Termination (Cal. FIN § 673)

Right to File a Claim for Damages

Following the termination of a lease, you have a right to file a claim for damages. Details regarding the value of this claim are provided in the documentation. (Cal. FIN § 673)

Notice to Quit Duration (Cal. CCP § 1161b)

90-day Notice before Property Removal

You should receive a 90-day notice before being removed from the property. Potential exceptions to this rule do exist. (Cal. CCP § 1161b)

Waiver and Estoppel (Cal. CIV § 1995.300)

Influential Factors

Rights and privileges can be subject to applicable defense. Terms of the lease agreement may play a pivotal role. (Cal. CIV § 1995.300)

Right to Notice of Termination from Owner (Cal. CIV § 1946.1)

60 Days’ Notice Requirement

You should receive a 60 days’ notice from the owner before lease termination. A different requirement is needed for less than a year of residence and for cases where the owner intends to sell the dwelling to a purchaser. (Cal. CIV § 1946.1)

Recovery from Lessee (Cal. CIV § 3308)

Right to Excess of Amount of Rent

You can recover excess rent after termination. This rule can affect your decision on seeking other remedies. (Cal. CIV § 3308)

Right to Continue Possession (Cal. CCP § 1161)

Right to Continue Occupation until Lease End

You have the right to continue occupying the property until the end of the lease term. (Cal. CCP § 1161)

Legal Action against Landlord (Cal. CIV § 1940.2)

Right to Legal Action

If there are any violations of your rights, you have the right to take legal action against the landlord. (Cal. CIV § 1940.2)

Termination of Lease Contract (Cal. CIV § 2987)

Right to Lease Termination

You can terminate the lease contract anytime before the scheduled expiration date. Various liabilities may apply in such a case. (Cal. CIV § 2987)

Right to Possess Property (Cal. CCP § 326)

Right to Continue Property Possession

You can continue having the right to possess the property until the expiration of the five-year period following the termination of the tenancy. (Cal. CCP § 326)

Joint and Several Liability (Cal. CIV § 1995.330)

Liabilities of Assignee

You can jointly sue the tenant and the assignee in case of violation of restriction transfer. (Cal. CIV § 1995.330)

Consent for the Transfer of Lease (Cal. CIV § 1995.250)

Requirement for Landlord’s Consent

Landlord’s consent may be required for the transfer of your interest in the lease. (Cal. CIV § 1995.250)

Compliance with Eviction or Displacement Laws (Cal. CIV § 2924n)

Owner’s Legal Duties

As an owner, the tenant needs to comply with applicable laws regarding eviction or displacement. (Cal. CIV § 2924n)

Recovery of Damages from Lessee (Cal. COM § 10527(b))

Right to Accrued and Unpaid Rent

A lessee holds the right to accrued and unpaid rent, among other things. (Cal. COM § 10527(b))

Notice of Disposition of Personal Property (Cal. CIV § 1993.03)

Notice Requirement Details

Then tenant should receive a written notice describing the remaining property and where to claim it. (Cal. CIV § 1993.03)

Rights or Remedies for the Tenant’s Default (Cal. COM § 10505)

Right to Pursue Remedies

Users may seek damages or other remedies for the tenant’s default. (Cal. COM § 10505)

Right to Reasonable Access to Property (Cal. CIV § 789.3)

Right to Access the Leased Property

You have a right to access the property. The landlord cannot prevent such access with measures like changing the locks. (Cal. CIV § 789.3)

Right to Not be Evicted Without Reasonable Cause (Cal. HSC § 34331)

Right to Written Statement of Cause

You have the right to a written statement explaining the reason for eviction. (Cal. HSC § 34331)

Exercise Other Rights Provided in Lease Contract (Cal. COM § 10523)

Right to Pursue Remedies in Lease Contract

You can exercise rights and pursue remedies provided in the lease contract. (Cal. COM § 10523)

Recovery of Certain Forms of Damages Following Breach (Cal. CIV § 1951.2)

Potential Damages Due to Lessee’s Breach

Damages that you can recover from the lessee following a breach and abandonment of property are outlined in the documentation. (Cal. CIV § 1951.2)

Right to Protections and Remedies (Cal. CIV § 1953)

Various Rights and Remedies As a Tenant

You hold various rights to protections and remedies as a tenant. (Cal. CIV § 1953)

Right to Vacate the Premises (Cal. CIV § 1942(a))

Right to Leave the Premises

You can vacate the premises following the landlord’s neglect to repair certain dilapidations. (Cal. CIV § 1942(a))

Right to Reclaim Abandoned Property (Cal. CIV § 1993.04)

Post-Eviction Rights

You may reclaim personal property left behind in the premises post-eviction. (Cal. CIV § 1993.04)

Right to Exercise Certain Acts without Retaliation (Cal. HSC § 17031.5)

Exercise of Legal Rights

You hold the right to exercise certain actions without facing retaliation from the landlord. (Cal. HSC § 17031.5)

Right to Nullify the Declaration of Forfeiture (Cal. CCP § 1161.5)

Details of Nullification

You can nullify the lease forfeiture by performing specified actions within three days after receiving a notice. (Cal. CCP § 1161.5)

Recovery of Damages Following Disaffirmance of Lease (Cal. FIN § 17641)

Right to File a Claim for Damages

Following the disaffirmance of a lease, you can file a claim for damages. (Cal. FIN § 17641)

Right to Present a Defense in an Unlawful Detainer Proceeding (Cal. GOV § 7060.6)

Right to Present Defense

In an unlawful detainer proceeding, you have the right to present a defense against the eviction. (Cal. GOV § 7060.6)

Sale of Mobilehome within the Park (Cal. CIV § 798.56a)

Notice of Intention to Dispose

If the property in question is a mobilehome, you have specific rights regarding its sale and disposition, including the right to continue leasing the site until it is resold. (Cal. CIV § 798.56a)

Lease Continuation (Cal. CIV § 1951.4)

Lease Status with Subletting or Assignment

If the lease allows for subletting or assignment, it continues to be in effect as long as the lessor does not terminate your right to possession. (Cal. CIV § 1951.4)

Relief Against Forfeiture of the Lease (Cal. CCP § 1179)

Possibility for Relief Against Lease Forfeiture

You could petition the court to restore you to your former estate or tenancy by showcasing hardship. The court has discretionary power to grant relief against forfeiture. (Cal. CCP § 1179)

Project Limitations

Reminder: Abe doesn't replace a licensed legal professional.

Abe isn't designed to:

  • Provide legal advice.
  • Recommend or suggest legal courses of action.
  • Be used as a legal defense.
  • Substitute a human lawyer, attorney, or any licensed legal professional.
  • Advise on conduct or speech in a legal court setting.

Abe's primary function is as an informational tool. It's currently in its prototype phase, but we're enthusiastic about its development and the goals ahead.

Behind the Scenes: How Abe Operates

By leveraging a comprehensive database that encompasses the full text of the California Legal Code, sourced from meticulous web scraping of official documents, Abe ensures accuracy. This data is subject to a detailed cleaning process, with pivotal features extracted and stored for quick and precise user query responses.

The infrastructure is grounded in Python 3.8.9 and encompasses technologies like PostgreSQL for database management and OpenAI for text embedding and smart search functions. As we advance, the goal is to incorporate functionalities from GPT 3.5 Turbo and potentially GPT 4.0 to further amplify Abe's capabilities.

The journey has just begun, but the roadmap is filled with promise. Our aim is to evolve Abe into a dependable tool for every citizen, granting them swift and accurate legal information.
